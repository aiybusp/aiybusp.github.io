Case Study: Digestive Upset ## Summary A 28-year-old patient presented to the ER with heartburn, worsening abdominal pain, and melena. Daily ibuprofen use for chronic shoulder pain was the only medication history. Initial and peer-supported reasoning pointed toward peptic ulcer disease (PUD) with upper gastrointestinal (GI) bleeding, most likely gastric or duodenal in origin. --- ### Patient Presentation | **Category** | **Details** | |--------------------------|-------------| | **Age/Sex** | 28 years old | | **Chief Complaint** | “Bad heartburn” and generalized abdominal pain, worsening over time | | **Associated Symptom** | Black stools during bowel movements (melena) | | **History of Present Illness** | Gradual worsening of abdominal discomfort; dark stools noticed recently | | **Past Medical History** | Chronic shoulder pain from skiing injury | | **Medications** | Daily ibuprofen (NSAID) | | **Allergies** | None reported | | **Other PMHx** | None reported | --- ### Initial Diagnosis & Reasoning (Ari Perkins) **Primary Diagnosis:** Peptic Ulcer Disease (likely gastric or duodenal) **Likely Structures Involved:** Stomach or proximal duodenum **Rationale:** - **NSAID Use:** Daily ibuprofen inhibits COX-1 and COX-2 → reduced prostaglandin synthesis → decreased mucosal protection → increased ulcer risk. - **Melena:** Strongly indicates upper GI bleeding from ulceration. - **Lack of Other Comorbidities:** Supports a drug-induced etiology. - **Next Steps:** Upper endoscopy (EGD) for visualization, H. pylori testing. --- ### Peer Discussion Highlights - **Consensus Diagnosis:** The majority of peers agreed with PUD + upper GI bleed due to chronic NSAID use. - **Alternative Differentials Raised:** Gastritis, esophagitis, or GERD. - **Common Management Points:** Discontinue NSAIDs, initiate PPI therapy, perform EGD, and test for H. pylori. --- ### Final Conclusion (Ari Perkins) After peer review, I maintained my initial diagnosis: **Peptic Ulcer Disease with Upper GI Bleed**. If the patient had presented with **red blood in the stool** (hematochezia) instead of melena, my differential would expand to include **lower GI sources** such as hemorrhoids, diverticulosis, colorectal cancer, or inflammatory bowel disease. **Additional Workup in that Scenario:** Colonoscopy in addition to upper endoscopy. --- ### References - Sostres, C., et al.
